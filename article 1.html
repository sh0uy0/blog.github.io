It is human nature to misunderstand and misinterpret information. Since my early campus days I always met fellow students and a conversation always ensued on what we were pursuing in school. Most of the people always thought of a kitchen, cooking and serving when I told them that I pursued nutrition. Well, there is more to it. There is a whole story of what happens to the food we eat and how it interacts with our body to sustain life. I would like help most of us demystify some of these notions we have as regards to this branch of science. Nutrition is a biological process by which substances in food are transformed into body tissues and provide energy for the full range of physical and mental activities that make up human life. As a nutritionist, our work is to now study the impact that the food we eat has on our health which boils down to the different body processes that out bodies carry out. The body requires fuel in which to run on and food is the major source of fuel in which it can run on. I mean, what would happen if we did not eat? Nutrition and good nutrition at that is inevitable.

An investment in knowledge pays the best interest.
Benjamin Franklin.

Diets are on the emergence in the world which we are living in. But do we really know what a diet is? A diet is the sum of foods in which we are consuming. Again, diets are not always for weight loss management or other reasons which we say we are dieting for. Since we consume foods everyday, we can comfortably say that we have a diet everyday. The difference between me and you is probably the food groups we incorporate into our diet and the portion sizes we have a liking for. Nutrients are the components in a food that we use to grow, survive and offer protection. We mostly find ourselves consuming a certain food because of the nutrients in the food. Essentially these nutrients in food are the reason why we eat. If we were to get them in these forms through other means then food wouldn’t have formed an important part of our living. We largely have micronutrients and micronutrients. Macronutrients are required in large quantities and micronutrients in lower quantities. Macronutrients mostly provide energy which is what we mostly need if we are to continue with our day to day activities. Energy is inevitable if our major body organs such as the liver, heart and kidney are to function and. And what better way to enhance that if we don’t take macronutrients. Micronutrients provide the necessary co-factors that facilitate metabolism. They are like accessories which facilitate the process of metabolism. I hope I have been able to inform and engage you in this conversation. We shall continue to educate each other more and grow healthier populations. Leave a comment, clarification or even question. As always, stay healthy.
